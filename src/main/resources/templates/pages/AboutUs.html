Founder
My name is Lisa Jewell, and I am a registered social worker with experience working with women fleeing domestic violence during their time of crisis. I have worked in the front lines in the social work field now for almost 9 years. I am proud of the work I have done in the community but would like to fill yet another huge gap in service. I have built a support group for women who have escaped domestic violence or experienced some kind heavy trauma. There are agencies here in town that work with them while in crisis and get them to safety, but after care for them is next to impossible due to the high demand of clients and limited staff specialized in this area. During discussions in the group, I have been asked to teach some of the classes and specialized topics that they would like to learn. I am happy to oblige this request but realized this would turn into an ongoing thing that people would need continuous learning of safety skills. Clients will not necessarily be discharged as I do expect them to want to engage in different classes and activities as they are offered. The mission is to provide women a safe space where they can receive education, relief, learned coping skills, become empowered with the radical acceptance of their own lives. To learn how to express their barriers that are stopping them from moving forward with their lives in a prosocial way.
